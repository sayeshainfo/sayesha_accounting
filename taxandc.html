TAX & COMPLIANCE

Tax and compliance services

Tax and compliance services encompass a range of offerings designed to assist individuals, businesses, and organizations in meeting their tax obligations and adhering to relevant regulations. These services are provided by experts in the field who possess an in-depth understanding of local, national, and international tax laws, ensuring that all financial activities are executed within legal boundaries.

GST FILING

Goods and Services Tax (GST) filing is a mandatory financial obligation for businesses in many countries that have implemented the GST system. This process involves reporting the details of your sales, purchases, and tax payments to the government.

GST RECONCILIATION

GST reconciliation is a critical process for businesses in countries that have implemented the Goods and Services Tax (GST) system. It involves comparing the tax data reported by the taxpayer with the data recorded by the tax authorities to ensure accuracy and compliance

GST CANCELLATION & REVOCATION

Goods and Services Tax (GST) Cancellation and Revocation are two distinct processes related to the GST system in many countries. These processes are initiated to manage the registration status of taxpayers.

Tax Planning

Tax planning is a strategic financial management process that individuals and businesses undertake to optimize their tax liabilities legally. The primary goal of tax planning is to minimize the amount of taxes paid while ensuring compliance with tax laws

Audits and Representations

Audits and representations are important aspects of financial and legal compliance for businesses and individuals. Audits involve the examination of financial records and processes to ensure accuracy and adherence to regulations, while representations involve legal or financial experts advocating on behalf of clients

Estate and Gift Tax Planning

Estate and gift tax planning are essential financial strategies aimed at managing the tax implications of wealth transfer, whether during one's lifetime or after death.

The GST Filing Process:

Obtaining GST Registration

Businesses exceeding the turnover threshold set by the tax authorities must register for GST. Registration provides a unique Goods and Services Tax Identification Number (GSTIN) necessary for all transactions.

Maintaining Records

Businesses are required to maintain detailed records of all transactions, including sales, purchases, and input tax credit claims.

Filing Returns

GST returns are filed periodically, summarizing the business's sales, purchases, and tax liabilities. The frequency of filing varies based on the type and size of the business.

Reconciliation

Businesses need to reconcile the data provided in their returns with their books of accounts, ensuring accuracy and consistency.

Payment of Tax

After filing the return, businesses are required to pay the tax liability as per the return's details.

Input Tax Credit:

Filing returns correctly allows businesses to claim input tax credit, reducing their overall tax liability.

The GST Reconciliation Process

Collecting Data

Gather all relevant financial data, including invoices, purchase orders, sales records, and GST returns filed.

Matching Invoices

Compare the invoices entered into your accounting system with the invoices reported in your GSTR-1 (outward supplies) and GSTR-2A (inward supplies).

Identifying Discrepancies

Identify discrepancies such as missing invoices, differences in values or tax amounts, duplicate entries, and non-matching GSTINs.

Rectification

Rectify errors in your accounting system and ensure that the accurate data is reflected in the returns you file.

Reconciling Input Tax Credit

Verify that the input tax credit claimed matches the eligible amount based on the invoices and GSTR-2A data.

Amendments

If errors are identified after filing returns, use the appropriate amendment forms to correct them in subsequent returns.

Process of GST Cancellation

Application Submission

The registered taxpayer needs to submit an application for GST cancellation through the GST portal.

Verification

The tax authorities review the application and may request additional information or documents for verification.

Cancellation Order

If the application is approved, the tax authorities issue a cancellation order, and the taxpayer's GSTIN is deactivated.

Return Filing

The taxpayer must file any pending GST returns up to the date of cancellation and pay any outstanding dues.
